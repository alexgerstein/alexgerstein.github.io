Here are some of the projects I've been able to share with others:

DARTPlan

While looking to learn languages and frameworks that are not covered in my CS courses, I decided to make a webapp that allows Dartmouth students to better plan out their majors. Before the app, students had to look up each course they wanted to take on a website and manually check for scheduling changes and conflicts. My Flask webapp does all of this trivial work by scraping the course pages and providing a simple, user-friendly interface to drag and drop courses during terms when they are offered. Over 1,450 students have already signed-up! The code is currently open-source.

mapd

This iPhone app allowed the Dartmouth community to post and share photos of events around campus. Two of my peers and I went from knowing nothing about Objective-C or XCode to finalizing this social networking app in only six weeks. I personally worked mainly on the front-end of the project.

SVN Comet


The four-week team project for Software Design and Implementation (COSC 50) required each group to make its own multiplayer physics game, á la Crayon Physics, in C using GTK and the Chipmunk Physics Library. This was the first project I developed with a team. Everyone stepped up tremendously, but the project management experience I gained from leading the team (two of the three other members were busy during crunch time with their senior theses) was invaluable. Early on in the process, I was tasked with the GUI, but as gaps formed in the work, I stepped up to assist with the client-server interaction and physics simulation.

]]>http://alexgerstein.com/projects/20919158-adad-484b-8436-0df860110706Thu, 10 Jul 2014 07:15:00 GMT<![CDATA[Films I've Directed]]>My greatest passion aside from technology is filmmaking. Making movies is a great way to collaborate with a team and to create something that extends beyond yourself.

Over the years, I have taken numerous courses in film and, in addition to my Computer Science major, am working towards a Film and Media Studies minor. Here are a couple of the student films I've created:

Good Vibes & Duct Tape: Stories with Cindy Pierce

For my documentary filmmaking class in Spring 2014, I directed this short about local mother/innkeeper/stand-up comedian, Cindy Pierce. At over 22 minutes, this was the longest film I've worked on by far. Thankfully, I did not work on this alone. Each of the six other students in the class had a hand in the final product. Not to mention the tremendous enthusiasm and kindness that Cindy brought to the film! In addition to the trailer above, you can watch behind-the-scenes footage edited by the cinematographer, Eddie Zapata. If you're interested in seeing the film in its entirety, let me know.

Why I Joined "The Dartmouth"

While spending my sophomore summer on campus--as sophomores are required to do at Dartmouth--I put together a recruitment video for The Dartmouth, our daily newspaper. This is unofficially the first video of the revamped The Dartmouth Multimedia staff. Since putting together a team of directors and editors, the staff has produced some great work that you can check out on our YouTube Channel.

Snoodles

The summer before my sophomore year of high school, I traveled on a National Geographic Student Expedition to Iceland. While there, we were tasked with directing a five-minute short about anything we found interesting. This is a character study I made about a sheep named Snoodles who pestered the patrons of the Cowshed Café in Vogafjosi.

Occupy Digibrain

As the culminating project for my freshman writing course (WRIT 5), I directed my friends in a sci-fi film--part 1984, part Occupy Wall Street--about the dangers of computer-assisted brains. It sounds tacky. It is...but I had a great time putting it together with them.

The Director and Me

I created this film as my final assignment for Filmmaking 1 in Fall 2012. We only had one week to write, film and edit, so the production schedule was tight for everyone. To work around this issue, I wrote a story that would only require one actor. The main actor actually has a twin brother, so I wasn't able to convince everyone that only one of them acted in this film.

Laughing through Modernization

I wanted to make my final paper for Japanese Cinema (FILM 42) more accessible to those who have not seen the film I analyzed, so instead of simply writing a paper on Yasuzo Masumura's underated gem, Giants and Toys (1958), I made a visual essay to accompany it.
